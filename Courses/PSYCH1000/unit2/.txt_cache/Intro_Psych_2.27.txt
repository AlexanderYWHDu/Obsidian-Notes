More caveats on operant conditioning: punishment
· Punishment can be tricky
· Punished behavior seems to be suppressed not forgotten · And only suppressed in certain situations! (teaches discrimination among
situations) · Punishment can teach fear of the punisher, inhibit learning new/better responses · Physical punishment may model violence (more on this soon) · Physical punishment may reduce the behavior, but what springs up in its place?
Punishment tells you what not to do; reinforcement tells you what to do

More caveats on operant conditioning: reinforcement
· The timing and schedule of the reinforcer matter
· Delayed vs. immediate · Continuous vs. partial (intermittent)
· Lots of flavors of intermittent but just know it gets you better results that continuous (more persistent learning; harder to extinguish)
· I give myself a little snacky SOMETIMES after an hour of studying
