And other induced states...
· Psychoactive drugs include chemicals that alter the brain, producing changes in perceptions and moods
· Depressants (e.g., alcohol, tranquilizers)
· Opioids/Narcotics (heroin, codeine) an especially dangerous type · Euphoria, pain relief · Calm neural activity and slow body functions: slowed processing, memory disruption,
disinhibition
· Stimulants (e.g., nicotine, methamphetamine)
· Alertness, appetite suppression · Excites neural activity, speeds body functions
· Hallucinogens/psychedelics (e.g., mushrooms, marijuana, LSD)
· Produce or distort sensation & perception

Drug use can become problematic...
· Tolerance: Need for increasingly larger doses of a drug to produce the same effect
Reduction in dopamine receptors in striatum
· Withdrawal: Discomfort and distress that follow the discontinuation of an addictive drug

Drug use can become problematic...

Substance use disorder: · Mild = 2-3 indicators · Moderate = 4-5 indicators · Severe = 6+ indicators
We generally don't use these terms for behavioral patterns without a chemical intoxicant component ("I'm addicted to
my phone") but there is definitely evidence that some behaviors can become excessive and problematic

Diminished Control 1. Uses more substance, or for longer, than intended. 2. Tries unsuccessfully to regulate use of substance. 3. Spends much time acquiring, using, or recovering from effects of substance. 4. Craves the substance. Diminished Social Functioning 5. Use disrupts commitments at work, school, or home. 6. Continues use despite social problems. 7. Causes reduced social, recreational, and work activities. Hazardous Use 8. Continues use despite hazards. 9. Continues use despite worsening physical or psychological problems. Drug Action 10. Experiences tolerance (needing more substance for the desired effect). 11. Experiences withdrawal (unpleasant mental or physical reactions) when attempting to end use.
