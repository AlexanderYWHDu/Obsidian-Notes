# Sleep Disorders & Dreaming (★★)

#sleep #sleep-disorders

## Overview Table

| Disorder | Description | Treatment |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| Insomnia | Ongoing difficulty falling/staying asleep | CBT; sleeping pills |
| Narcolepsy | Sudden attacks of overwhelming sleepiness | Stimulants |
| Parasomnias | Abnormal behaviors during sleep transitions | Varies |

---

## Major Sleep Disorders

### Insomnia
- Persistent difficulty falling or **staying** asleep
- Leads to chronic tiredness; impairs cognitive performance
- **First-line treatment**: Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) — more effective than pills long-term
- Secondary option: sleeping pills

### Narcolepsy
- **Sudden, uncontrollable attacks of sleepiness** — can fall asleep mid-activity
- Risk: falling asleep in dangerous situations (driving, operating machinery)
- Treatment: stimulant medications

---

## Parasomnias
> Abnormal experiences or behaviors **during sleep or during transitions** in/out of sleep

| Parasomnia | Key Facts |
|------------|-----------|
| **Sleepwalking (Somnambulism)** | NOT associated with dreaming; no memory; not dangerous to wake |
| **REM Sleep Behavior Disorder** | Dream-enacting behaviors (speaking, hitting, moving) |
| **Sleep-Related Eating Disorder (SRED)** | Eating/drinking while asleep; no or partial memory next day |

> [!warning] Sleepwalking vs. REM Behavior Disorder
> - **Sleepwalking** = occurs in **N-REM** (not dreaming); person acts without dreaming
> - **REM Sleep Behavior Disorder** = occurs during **REM**; person physically acts out their dreams (because normal REM paralysis fails)

### Legal Case: Kenneth Parks
- Canadian man acquitted of murder because of **"homicidal sleepwalking"**
- Legal concept: **Automatism** — unaware of actions when committing the act

---

## Dreaming

- Occurs mainly during **REM** sleep
- Why do we dream? Researchers are genuinely uncertain:
  1. Dreams help process and make sense of the day's events
  2. Dreams are random neural activity that we construct meaning from
  3. Probably some combination of both

## Exam/Test Patterns

| Scenario/Keyword | Answer |
|-----------------|--------|
| Best treatment for insomnia (first-line) | **CBT** (not sleeping pills) |
| Sudden daytime sleep attacks | **Narcolepsy** → treated with stimulants |
| Sleepwalking: associated with dreaming? | **No** — occurs in N-REM |
| Acts out dreams, hits partner during sleep | **REM Sleep Behavior Disorder** |
| No memory of eating crackers at 3am | **Sleep-Related Eating Disorder (SRED)** |
