# Consciousness & Disorders (★★★)

#consciousness

## Overview Table

| Concept | Key Point |
|---------|-----------|
| Consciousness | Subjective awareness of self + environment |
| Persistent Vegetative State | Wakefulness without awareness |

## Definition of Consciousness
- **Consciousness** = our subjective awareness of ourselves and our environment

```
SPECTRUM OF CONSCIOUS AWARENESS
Full consciousness ←──────────────────────────────→ No consciousness
Normal          Minimally     Vegetative     Coma / Deep
waking          Conscious     State          anesthesia
                State
```

## Disordered States of Consciousness

### Persistent Vegetative State (PVS)
- Wakefulness **without** awareness
- Classic case: **Terri Schiavo**
- Brain imaging challenge: vegetative patients' brains sometimes show activity patterns similar to healthy people when asked to imagine playing tennis or navigating home → we may be **inaccurate** at the clinical distinction

### Minimally Conscious State (MCS)
- **Some awareness**; transitions between normal consciousness and deep coma
- Classic case: **Jan Grzebski** (19-year coma, regained consciousness)

### Locked-in Syndrome
- "Normal" consciousness with **minimal ability to show it**
- Classic case: **Jean-Dominique Bauby** — communicated by blinking one eye

> [!warning] Clinical Caution
> These three states can be difficult to distinguish clinically. Brain imaging (fMRI tasks) may reveal hidden awareness in vegetative patients.

## Disorders of Visual Consciousness

### Blindsight
- Cause: Damage to **primary visual cortex**
- Effect: **Perception without consciousness** — patient denies seeing but their hand reaches correctly, avoids obstacles
- Patient attributes responses to "feelings" not vision

### Unilateral Neglect
- Cause: Typically injury to **right parietal lobe**
- Effect: Impairment attending to **left side of space**
- **NOT a vision problem** — it's an *attention/consciousness* problem
- Example: Anton Raederscheidt's self-portraits during stroke recovery showed only the right half of his face initially

> [!important] Blindsight vs. Neglect
> | | Blindsight | Unilateral Neglect |
> |---|---|---|
> | Brain area | Primary visual cortex | Right parietal lobe |
> | Issue | No conscious visual experience | Cannot attend to left space |
> | Vision intact? | No (damaged) | Yes (vision is fine) |

---

## Exam/Test Patterns

| Scenario/Keyword | Answer |
|-----------------|--------|
| Patient reaches for object but says they can't see it | **Blindsight** |
| Patient draws only right side of a clock | **Unilateral Neglect** |
| "Normal" awareness, cannot move | **Locked-in Syndrome** |
| Wakefulness, no awareness, some brain activation to mental imagery | **Vegetative State** (possibly hidden awareness) |
| Which disorder is NOT a consciousness disorder? | **Unilateral Neglect** (it's an attention disorder) |
