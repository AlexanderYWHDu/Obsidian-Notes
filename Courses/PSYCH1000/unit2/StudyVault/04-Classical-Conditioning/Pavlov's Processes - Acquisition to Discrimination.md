# Pavlov's Processes — Acquisition to Discrimination (★★★)

#classical-conditioning #learning

## Overview Table

| Process | Definition |
|---------|-----------|
| Acquisition | Initial learning stage; NS + US repeated until NS → CR |
| Extinction | CS without US → CR diminishes |
| Spontaneous Recovery | CR reappears after rest following extinction |
| Generalization | Similar stimuli to CS also elicit CR |
| Discrimination | Learning to distinguish CS from other similar stimuli |

---

## The 5 Processes

### 1. Acquisition
- The initial phase where NS is repeatedly paired with US
- **Works best when**:
  - NS **precedes** the US (not simultaneous or after)
  - NS is **close in time** to US
  - There are **many pairings** (trials)

### 2. Extinction
- CS is presented repeatedly **without** the US → CR gradually diminishes
- ⚠️ Extinction does **NOT** destroy the original learning — the association still exists

### 3. Spontaneous Recovery
- After extinction + a **rest period**, the CR **reappears** (usually weakened)
- Proves that extinction ≠ erasing the original memory

```
ACQUISITION/EXTINCTION/RECOVERY TIMELINE:

Strength of CR
    ▲
    │  ████
    │ █    █
    │█      █         Recovery
    │        ████      ▲▲
    │            ▓▓▓  ▲  ▓▓▓
    │               ▓▓    ▓▓▓
    └──────────────────────────► Time
       Acquisition  Extinction  Rest  Spontaneous
```

### 4. Generalization
- Once conditioned, **stimuli similar to CS** also elicit similar responses
- Example: **Little Albert** — conditioned to fear white rat, also feared white rabbit, fur coat, Santa mask

### 5. Discrimination
- Learning to **distinguish** the CS from other similar stimuli that are NOT paired with US
- Example: Alarm at gym (different context) → no grumpy response

---

## Example: Little Albert (Watson & Rayner)
- Demonstrated emotional conditioning in humans
- Loud noise (US) → fear (UR)
- White rat (NS) + loud noise → white rat (CS) → fear (CR)
- **Generalization**: Albert feared other white, furry objects

---

## Alarm Clock Example

| Stage | What happens |
|-------|-------------|
| Acquisition | Alarm + grogginess (many mornings) → alarm alone → grumpy |
| Extinction | Alarm rings but no more early wake-up obligation → grumpy response fades |
| Spontaneous Recovery | After a vacation, the alarm rings → mild grumpy feeling returns |
| Generalization | Different alarm sound → also grumpy |
| Discrimination | Gym timer alarm → no grumpy (different context) |

## Exam/Test Patterns

| Scenario/Keyword | Answer |
|-----------------|--------|
| CS repeatedly without US → response fades | **Extinction** |
| After extinction + break → CR returns briefly | **Spontaneous Recovery** |
| Does extinction erase the original learning? | **No** — association remains |
| Fear of white rat spreads to white rabbit | **Generalization** |
| Only fear this specific alarm, not others | **Discrimination** |
| Acquisition works best when NS ___ US | NS **precedes** US, close in time |
