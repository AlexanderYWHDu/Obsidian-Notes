# Classical Conditioning Basics (★★★)

#classical-conditioning #learning

## Overview Table

| Term | Abbreviation | Definition |
|------|-------------|-----------|
| Unconditioned Stimulus | **US** | Naturally & automatically triggers a response |
| Unconditioned Response | **UR** | Natural, automatic response to US |
| Neutral Stimulus | **NS** | Elicits no response before conditioning |
| Conditioned Stimulus | **CS** | Former NS; triggers response after pairing with US |
| Conditioned Response | **CR** | Learned response to the CS |

---

## What is Classical Conditioning?

- **Definition**: Learning in which an organism comes to associate stimuli — a neutral stimulus comes to trigger a response originally caused by another stimulus
- Discovered by **Ivan Pavlov** studying dog digestion
- Part of **associative learning**: we learn to expect and prepare for significant events

> [!tip] Behaviorism Context
> Behaviorists insisted on only measuring observable, quantifiable behavior. Classical conditioning fits this framework: Stimulus → Response.

---

## Pavlov's Experiment

```
BEFORE CONDITIONING:
US (food) ──────────────────► UR (salivation)
NS (bell) ──────────────────► no response

DURING CONDITIONING (acquisition):
NS (bell) + US (food) ──────► UR (salivation)
         [repeated pairings]

AFTER CONDITIONING:
CS (bell) ──────────────────► CR (salivation)
```

> [!important] CS ≠ NS; CR ≠ UR
> - Once the NS reliably triggers a response, it becomes the **CS**
> - The **CR** is typically **weaker** than the UR (the learned response is usually less strong than the natural one)

---

## Higher-Order Conditioning
- A CS can be paired with a new NS to create a second CS
- Example: bell (CS₁) → food (US). Then light (NS) + bell (CS₁) → eventually light alone triggers salivation (CR)

## Pavlov's Alarm Clock Example (Mapped)

| Element | Example |
|---------|---------|
| US | Waking up early (alarm + groggy routine) |
| UR | Feeling grumpy |
| NS → CS | Alarm sound |
| CR | Feeling grumpy just from hearing the alarm |

---

## Exam/Test Patterns

| Scenario/Keyword | Answer |
|-----------------|--------|
| Before conditioning: bell → no response | Bell is a **NS** |
| After conditioning: bell → salivation | Bell is now **CS**; salivation is **CR** |
| Food → salivation (always, naturally) | Food = **US**; salivation = **UR** |
| Is CR stronger or weaker than UR? | **Weaker** |
| Can you condition to a CS? | Yes — **higher-order conditioning** |
