# Observational Learning (★★)

#observational-learning #learning

## Overview Table

| Concept | Key Point |
|---------|-----------|
| Observational Learning | Learning by watching others |
| Modeling | Imitating a specific observed behavior |
| Vicarious Reinforcement | Learning increases by seeing others rewarded |
| Vicarious Punishment | Learning decreases by seeing others punished |
| Latent Learning | Learning that occurs without obvious reinforcement; revealed later |

---

## Observational Learning (Social Learning)

- **Definition**: Learning new behaviors by **observing** events, other people, and through language
- We can learn things we have **neither experienced nor directly observed**
- Goes beyond behaviorism — requires cognitive processes (attention, memory, imitation)

### Modeling
- **Definition**: The process of observing and **imitating** a specific behavior
- Key researcher: **Albert Bandura**

---

## Bandura's Bobo Doll Study (1963)

```
Condition A: Children watch adult model act AGGRESSIVELY toward Bobo doll
Condition B: Children watch adult model act NON-aggressively
Condition C: No model (control)

Result: Children who saw aggression showed more aggression
→ "Vicarious reinforcement" of aggression
```

- **Key finding**: Children **imitated aggressive behavior** after observing an adult model demonstrate it
- Implies that **media violence** may model aggressive behavior in children

---

## Vicarious Reinforcement & Punishment

- **Vicarious Reinforcement**: You increase a behavior after seeing *someone else* get rewarded for it
- **Vicarious Punishment**: You decrease a behavior after seeing *someone else* get punished for it
- No direct reinforcement/punishment required — observation is sufficient

---

## Latent Learning

- **Definition**: Learning that occurs but is **not demonstrated** until there is an incentive
- Challenges pure behaviorism — organisms learn even without direct reinforcement
- Example: Rats who explored a maze without reward still showed they had learned it when food was introduced
- Suggests cognition and mental maps are involved in learning

---

## Classical vs. Operant vs. Observational

| | Classical | Operant | Observational |
|---|---|---|---|
| Behavior type | Involuntary, reflexive | Voluntary | Voluntary (imitated) |
| Key mechanism | Stimulus–stimulus association | Behavior–consequence | Observation + imitation |
| Reinforcement needed? | No | Yes | No (vicarious is enough) |
| Key figure | Pavlov | Skinner | Bandura |

## Exam/Test Patterns

| Scenario/Keyword | Answer |
|-----------------|--------|
| Watch someone win and start doing the same thing | **Vicarious reinforcement** |
| Learning occurs even without reward | **Latent learning** |
| Child imitates TV violence | **Observational learning / modeling** |
| Bandura's study showed ___ | Children model **aggression** after observing aggressive adults |
| Does observational learning require direct reinforcement? | **No** — watching is enough |
