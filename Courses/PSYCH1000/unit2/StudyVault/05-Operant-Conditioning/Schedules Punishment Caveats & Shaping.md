# Schedules, Punishment Caveats & Shaping (★★)

#operant-conditioning #learning

## Overview Table

| Concept | Key Point |
|---------|-----------|
| Continuous Reinforcement | Every correct response reinforced; fast learning, fast extinction |
| Intermittent (Partial) Reinforcement | Not every response reinforced; slower learning, **very hard to extinguish** |
| Punishment Limitations | Suppresses behavior, doesn't eliminate; teaches what NOT to do |
| Shaping | Successive approximations toward goal behavior |

---

## Schedules of Reinforcement

### Continuous Reinforcement
- Reinforce **every** correct response
- Result: **Fast learning** but also **faster extinction** once reinforcer is removed

### Intermittent (Partial) Reinforcement
- Reinforce **sometimes** (not every response)
- Result: **More persistent** learning; **harder to extinguish**
- Why: When the reinforcer disappears, the organism keeps trying because it's used to not always getting a reward

> [!tip] Real-Life Example
> Slot machines use intermittent reinforcement — that's why gambling is so hard to stop.
> "I give myself a little snacky *sometimes* after an hour of studying" — more durable study habit than always rewarding.

```
SCHEDULE COMPARISON:
              | Learning Speed | Extinction Speed
Continuous    | Fast           | Fast
Intermittent  | Slower         | Very Slow (hard to extinguish)
```

---

## Timing of Reinforcement

- **Immediate** reinforcement is far more effective than **delayed**
- The closer in time the reinforcer follows the behavior, the stronger the learning

---

## Punishment Caveats

> [!warning] Why Punishment is Tricky
> 1. Punished behavior is **suppressed, not forgotten** — it often returns in different contexts
> 2. Punishment only works in **certain situations** — teaches discrimination, not elimination
> 3. Can teach **fear of the punisher**, not just the behavior
> 4. Inhibits learning new/better responses (doesn't tell you WHAT to do)
> 5. **Physical punishment** may **model violence** for children
> 6. Physical punishment reduces target behavior but often creates other problem behaviors

> **Key contrast**: Punishment tells you what NOT to do. Reinforcement tells you WHAT to do.

---

## Shaping

- **Definition**: Reinforcing **successive approximations** — step-by-step progress toward a desired final behavior
- Used when the target behavior doesn't naturally occur
- Example: Teaching a dog to roll over
  1. Reinforce lying down → 2. Reinforce partial roll → 3. Reinforce full roll

---

## Exam/Test Patterns

| Scenario/Keyword | Answer |
|-----------------|--------|
| Reinforce every response → stop reinforcing → quick stop | **Continuous reinforcement → fast extinction** |
| Reinforce occasionally → very hard to stop behavior | **Intermittent reinforcement** |
| Gambling behavior is so persistent because... | **Intermittent schedule** |
| Child stops misbehaving in front of parent but not others | **Punishment suppresses** (doesn't eliminate) behavior |
| Training dog step by step | **Shaping** via successive approximations |
| Which is more effective: delayed or immediate reinforcement? | **Immediate** |
