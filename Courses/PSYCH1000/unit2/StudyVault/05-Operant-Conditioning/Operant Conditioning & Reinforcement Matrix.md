# Operant Conditioning & Reinforcement Matrix (★★★)

#operant-conditioning #learning

## Overview Table

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Operant Conditioning | Voluntary behavior strengthened/weakened by consequences |
| Reinforcement | Consequence that **increases** behavior frequency |
| Punishment | Consequence that **decreases** behavior frequency |
| Positive | **Add** a stimulus |
| Negative | **Remove** a stimulus |

---

## The 4-Cell Matrix

> [!important] Critical Rule
> **Positive/Negative = Add/Remove** (NOT good/bad)
> **Reinforcement/Punishment = Increases/Decreases** behavior

| | **Present/Add (+)** | **Remove (−)** |
|---|---|---|
| **↑ Increases behavior** | **Positive Reinforcement** — add pleasant stimulus | **Negative Reinforcement** — remove unpleasant stimulus |
| **↓ Decreases behavior** | **Positive Punishment** — add unpleasant stimulus | **Negative Punishment** — remove pleasant stimulus |

---

## Each Cell Explained

### ✅ Positive Reinforcement
- Add a **pleasant** stimulus → behavior increases
- Examples:
  - Give yourself bubble tea after studying → more studying
  - Praise from a teacher → more hand-raising
  - Paycheck → continued work

### ✅ Negative Reinforcement
- Remove an **unpleasant** stimulus → behavior increases (provides **relief**)
- Examples:
  - Seatbelt beep stops when belt is on → more seatbelt use
  - Take ibuprofen → headache gone → more ibuprofen use
  - OCD compulsion → relieves obsession feeling → more compulsive behavior

> [!warning] Negative Reinforcement ≠ Punishment
> Negative Reinforcement INCREASES behavior. It's still reinforcement — just via removal of something bad.

### ⚠️ Positive Punishment
- Add an **unpleasant** stimulus → behavior decreases
- Examples:
  - Speeding ticket → less speeding
  - Snapping a rubber band on wrist when distracted → less phone use

### ⚠️ Negative Punishment
- Remove a **pleasant** stimulus → behavior decreases
- Examples:
  - Take away iPad when child misbehaves → less misbehavior
  - Delete a favorite app every time you check phone → less phone checking

---

## Skinner's Key Insight
- **Consequences** are the most important determinant of future behavior
- Developed the **operant conditioning chamber (Skinner box)** to study voluntary behavior in animals

### Shaping
- **Definition**: Gradually guiding behavior toward the desired behavior
- **Method**: Reinforcing **successive approximations** — closer and closer steps toward the target behavior
- Example: Training a dog to roll over starts with rewarding lying down, then a partial roll, then a full roll

## Exam/Test Patterns

| Scenario/Keyword | Answer |
|-----------------|--------|
| Car beep stops when you buckle → more buckling | **Negative Reinforcement** |
| Get a treat for good work → do more good work | **Positive Reinforcement** |
| Lose privileges for bad behavior → less bad behavior | **Negative Punishment** |
| Get a fine for bad behavior → less bad behavior | **Positive Punishment** |
| Superstitions (random reward → repeated behavior) | Result of accidental **Positive Reinforcement** |
| Training step-by-step toward goal | **Shaping** via **successive approximations** |
