## Question 1 - Drug Classification [recall]

> Classify each: (a) heroin, (b) alcohol, (c) LSD, (d) methamphetamine

> [!answer]- 정답 보기
> - (a) Heroin → **Opioid/Narcotic** (euphoria, pain relief, slows body)
> - (b) Alcohol → **Depressant** (slows neural activity, disinhibition)
> - (c) LSD → **Hallucinogen/Psychedelic** (distorts sensation and perception)
> - (d) Methamphetamine → **Stimulant** (excites neural activity, alertness)

## Question 2 - Tolerance [recall]

> Explain tolerance and its neurological mechanism.

> [!answer]- 정답 보기
> **Tolerance** = needing increasingly larger doses of a drug to produce the same effect. Mechanism: repeated drug use causes the brain to **reduce dopamine receptors in the striatum** — the reward circuitry becomes less sensitive, requiring more drug to achieve the same dopaminergic response.

## Question 3 - SUD Criteria [application]

> A person: (1) uses more alcohol than intended, (2) keeps trying to cut back but fails, (3) is missing work due to hangovers, (4) keeps drinking despite knowing it's worsening their liver disease, (5) feels shaky and anxious when they don't drink. How many SUD criteria are met, and what is the severity level?

> [!answer]- 정답 보기
> **5 criteria** met: (1) uses more than intended, (2) unsuccessful regulation, (5) disrupts work commitments, (9) continues despite worsening physical problem, (11) withdrawal symptoms. Severity = **Moderate SUD** (4–5 criteria).

## Question 4 - Hypnosis [recall]

> What are the key features of hypnosis, and what is the most well-supported clinical application?

> [!answer]- 정답 보기
> Hypnosis involves: **focused attention**, reduced peripheral awareness, and **enhanced suggestibility**. The proposed mechanism is **dissociation** (split in consciousness). The most well-supported clinical application is **pain reduction** (Patterson et al., 1992 — significant reduction in burn patient pain ratings).

---

## Question 5 - Hypnosis & Memory [application]

> A detective suggests using hypnosis to help a witness "recover" forgotten details about a crime. What concern should a psychologist raise?

> [!answer]- 정답 보기
> Hypnosis **increases suggestibility** — the enhanced suggestible state means that any leading questions or the hypnotist's expectations can be **implanted as false memories** rather than recovered as accurate ones. Research shows suggestive procedures create false memories in 20–30% of participants. Using hypnosis for memory recovery in legal contexts risks contaminating testimony.

## Question 6 - Meditation [recall]

> List three evidence-based benefits of meditation.

> [!answer]- 정답 보기
> 1. **Clinical reduction** in symptoms of some mental health conditions (anxiety, depression)
> 2. **Reduced stress and blood pressure**
> 3. **Improved attentional control**
> (Bonus: differential/structural brain activity changes, including in prefrontal cortex)

## Question 7 - SUD Classification [recall]

> Why do psychologists generally avoid calling social media use an "addiction" in the clinical sense?

> [!answer]- 정답 보기
> The term **Substance Use Disorder** (SUD) is technically reserved for conditions involving **chemical intoxicants** — substances that produce physiological tolerance and withdrawal. Social media overuse can be excessive and problematic, but it does not produce the same neurochemical dependency profile. That said, there IS evidence that some behaviors can become compulsive and harmful (internal Meta/Google documents acknowledge this).

## Question 8 - Analysis [analysis]

> Compare the recovery rate for Substance Use Disorder to other chronic conditions. What does this comparison suggest about how we should view SUD?

> [!answer]- 정답 보기
> SUD has a **relapse rate of 40–60%**, which is comparable to other chronic conditions like hypertension and diabetes. This comparison suggests that: (1) SUD is a **medical condition**, not a moral failing; (2) relapse is expected and manageable, not a sign of treatment failure; (3) long-term recovery is attainable with appropriate treatment. Viewing SUD as a "highly treatable disease" reduces stigma and encourages help-seeking.
