# Hypnosis & Meditation (★)

---

## Hypnosis

- **Definition**: Suggestion by a hypnotist of behaviors, feelings, or perceptions; involves **focused attention**, reduced peripheral awareness, enhanced suggestibility
- Mechanism debated: possibly **dissociation** — a split in consciousness where one part responds to suggestion while another observes

### Clinical Applications
- Quitting smoking / losing weight
- Recovering memories (controversial — risk of false memories)
- **Pain reduction** (Patterson et al., 1992 — significant pain rating reduction in burn patients)

> [!warning] Hypnosis & Memory Recovery
> Using hypnosis to recover memories is controversial because hypnosis may *increase* suggestibility and create false memories rather than retrieve accurate ones.

---

## Meditation

- **Definition**: Practices cultivating mindful, non-judgmental awareness of the present moment
- **Benefits**:
  - Clinical reduction in symptoms of some mental health conditions
  - Reduced stress and blood pressure
  - Improved attentional control
  - Differential brain activity (structural changes in prefrontal cortex)
