---

## Drug Categories

### Depressants
- **Effect**: Calm neural activity, slow body functions
- **Consequences**: Slowed processing, memory disruption, **disinhibition** (reduced restraint)
- Examples: alcohol, benzodiazepines (Xanax), barbiturates

### Opioids / Narcotics
- **Effect**: Bind to opioid receptors → euphoria, **pain relief**
- Especially dangerous due to overdose risk and rapid tolerance development
- Examples: heroin, codeine, morphine, fentanyl
- Context: **Opioid crisis** — overdose indicators recently trending in better direction

### Stimulants
- **Effect**: Excite neural activity, increase alertness, suppress appetite
- Examples: nicotine, caffeine, cocaine, methamphetamine, MDMA

### Hallucinogens / Psychedelics
- **Effect**: Produce or **distort** sensation and perception
- Examples: psilocybin (mushrooms), LSD, DMT, marijuana

---

## Tolerance & Withdrawal

```
DRUG USE CYCLE
Regular use → Brain adapts (e.g., reduces dopamine receptors in striatum)
           → Same dose = less effect = TOLERANCE
           → Need more drug for same effect
           → Stop drug = WITHDRAWAL (discomfort, distress)
```

- **Tolerance**: need for increasingly **larger doses** to produce the same effect
- **Withdrawal**: discomfort and distress following discontinuation
- Mechanism: reduction in **dopamine receptors in the striatum**

## Substance Use Disorder (SUD)

> [!important] SUD Severity Levels
> - **Mild**: 2–3 criteria met
> - **Moderate**: 4–5 criteria met
> - **Severe**: 6+ criteria met

### 11 Diagnostic Criteria (DSM-5)

**Diminished Control (1–4)**
1. Uses more substance, or for longer, than intended
2. Tries unsuccessfully to regulate use
3. Spends much time acquiring, using, or recovering from effects
4. Craves the substance

**Diminished Social Functioning (5–7)**

5. Use disrupts commitments at work, school, or home
6. Continues use despite social problems
7. Causes reduced social, recreational, and work activities

**Hazardous Use (8–9)**

8. Continues use despite hazards
9. Continues use despite worsening physical/psychological problems

**Drug Action (10–11)**

10. Experiences **tolerance**
11. Experiences **withdrawal**

---

## Key Facts

- SUD is a **highly treatable disease**; recovery is attainable
- **Relapse rate**: 40–60% — comparable to other chronic conditions (not a moral failing)
- "Behavioral addictions" (social media, gaming) share features but the term "SUD" is typically reserved for chemical intoxicants

## Exam/Test Patterns

| Scenario/Keyword | Answer |
|-----------------|--------|
| Needs 3 drinks where 1 used to work | **Tolerance** |
| Shaking and anxiety when stopping alcohol | **Withdrawal** |
| Alcohol class | **Depressant** |
| Pain relief + euphoria + slow body | **Opioid/Narcotic** |
| 5 SUD criteria met | **Moderate** SUD |
| Drug that distorts perception | **Hallucinogen** |
